Mastering Backend Development With NodeJS and ExpressJS

Published on Oct 9, 2023 at 10:00 AM

After years of crafting roadmaps and guiding junior developers, I've put together an easy-to-follow backend roadmap. Too much information can be overwhelming, especially for someone new to the field, so this guide is tailored to include just the essentials. The idea is simple: start with the necessary basics and delve into the nitty-gritty details as you progress and when they become relevant.

It's worth mentioning that this roadmap isn't for complete beginners to coding. Before embarking on this journey, you should have a basic grasp of JavaScript and, at the very least, know what NodeJS and ExpressJS areâ€”by "know," I simply mean being aware of their existence and basic purpose.

Each section of the roadmap breaks topics down based on subject matter, making them easily searchable. As a method of measurement, when you're learning each topic, challenge yourself with this question: "Can I explain this to someone else?" If the answer is yes, then you've truly grasped the concept. This approach ensures you gain a solid understanding before moving on to the next topic, eliminating unnecessary complexities.

By the end of this roadmap, you'll be equipped to craft dynamic applications where users can read, write, and delete data with confidence.

Roadmap

Goal: Build a fully functioning app

Pre-requisites:

  • Ability to create simple websites using HTML & CSS.
  • JavaScript Fundamentals: A solid understanding of:
    • Variables
    • Loops
    • Functions
    • DataTypes
    • Conditionals
    • DOM Manipulation (e.g., creating simple apps or navbars)
  • Advanced JavaScript Concepts: Comfort with:
    • Asynchronous JavaScript
    • Classes and Objects
    • Modules
  • Backend Basics: Familiarity with NodeJS and ExpressJS, including recognition of terms like:
    • Routes
    • Middleware
    • Controllers
    • Models

  • Design What You Want to Build:
    1. Decide what you want to build.
      • The app must be simple enough that you can picture it and imagine how your flow chart and database tables are going to be.
    2. Draw a flow chart.
      • The flow chart should include all the pages of your app and how they are connected.
    3. Design data models.
      • Design the App's database tables and ERDs (Entity Relationship Diagrams).
    4. [Optional] Create a Sequence Diagram for your app.
      • This is optional, but it is worth doing.
    5. Design the UI (User Interface).
      • Design the UI of your app using Figma or any other software or even pen and paper.
      • Your design must include all the pages of your app with full details based on your flow chart and diagrams.
