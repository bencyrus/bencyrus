JayPlus

JayPlus is a specialized business management software designed for car detailing businesses. Through this web application, businesses can let their customers book service appointments via their unique subdomains and seamlessly manage operations from the admin dashboard. Its current capabilities prominently feature robust booking and management tools.

  • Inspiration

    (February 2022)

    During my part-time job at a car detailing shop, I learned a ton about the business, especially since the owner, Moe, was a friend of mine. I quickly spotted several inefficiencies. Sometimes we had customers waiting, but no detailers available; other times, it was the opposite. Moe struggled with four different apps to run the business and two more just for bookings. Unfortunately, none of them really fit the scheduling needs of a detailing business. Around this time, Damon, my cousin and teammate on the project, asked about diving into UX Design. I had already given him some pointers on where to start. Seeing an opportunity, I brought him into the fold, sharing the issues I'd noticed at the shop, and together, we brainstormed solutions.

  • Design & Prototype

    (April 2022)

    After Damon completed the initial prototype, I was genuinely impressed, prompting me to commit to the project wholeheartedly. We presented the prototype to Moe, whose positive feedback confirmed our belief in its potential. To make it even better, we refined it based on additional feedback gathered from interviews with Moe and his customers.

  • MVP Challenges

    (September 2022)

    After finalizing the prototype, we began developing the MVP. At that time, I was juggling additional courses at school, so I enlisted my brother—who's skilled with PHP—to help build the app. Keen to get a functioning MVP quickly, we chose Laravel and LiveWire. I mapped out a detailed work breakdown structure to kick off the building process. While we successfully integrated the booking system, we faced hurdles with payment, SMS, and several other features due to LiveWire's limited documentation and my unfamiliarity with the platform. This led us to consider a complete rewrite.

  • Tech Pivot & Learning

    (November 2022)

    My brother and I were keen to learn Golang, and since I was already familiar with React, I took on the frontend while he handled the backend in Golang. We rebooted the project, laying out detailed tasks. He quickly developed the backend, outpacing my frontend work. This meant we had a backend ready but no frontend to test it against. When I did finish the frontend, we encountered several glitches. By then, my brother had joined a startup and had little time for our project. That pushed me to dive into Golang myself, and I spent several months on various projects before revisiting JayPlus.

  • Restructuring & Focus

    (May 2023)

    I was frustrated with the prolonged development of the project, so I dedicated myself until May to learn about project and product management. Additionally, starting my role as a test automation engineer in March, I gained significant insights into integration and testing.

    Wanting clarity on the end-goal, I drafted a detailed specification document. Influenced by the works of Robert C. Martin and Martin Fowler, I had a keen interest in clean code and architecture. While I contemplated between microservices and a monolithic setup, I wasn't swayed by the current trends. What appealed to me about microservices was the distinct separation of responsibilities and the capacity for independent teams to manage separate services. However, I recognized that my application's functions needed cohesion and that scaling wouldn't necessitate isolated service deployments based on potential traffic. This realization led me to merge the Domain Driven Design approach of microservices into a modular monolithic structure. My specification document, hence, began with a tech overview and delved deep into each module, backend routes, and frontend components. I crafted it meticulously, driven by my determination to ensure the project's success this time around.

  • Infrastructure and Basics

    (June 2023)

    I realized that if I wanted anyone to easily contribute to the app, setting up CICD was essential. Even though I'd sidestepped this for four years, the time had come. After diving into Docker and exploring various deployment methods, I chose to purchase a VM from DigitalOcean and integrate CI via GitHub Actions. It was a milestone moment for me; I'd completed numerous projects in the past, but this was the first where I could simply share my domain and grant someone instant access to the app – it felt distinct from just sharing static websites.

    For this phase, I started with the backend basics. I established the architecture, devised the modules, and initialized routes and handlers, mainly using GET requests to relay placeholder data that would later be replaced. When it came to the frontend, I took my foundational knowledge and, having gained deeper insights into React, I revamped the entire interface. This involved leveraging custom hooks, crafting efficient utility functions, and rectifying my initial over-reliance on useEffects.

  • Backend Deep Dive

    (August 2023)

    Once the frontend was nearly complete, I dove into backend development. I invested in a managed Postgres database and began shaping my models, crafting an ERD, and setting up handlers and queries. I encountered a significant challenge with the architecture I had chosen: Go's prohibition of cyclical dependencies. This was a curveball for me, leading to the integration of interfaces and passing the database as a dependency to the application. Initially, the backend took just a few days, but the nuances soon surfaced. To handle subdomains and SSL, I honed my skills with NginX. While integrating Stripe for payments and Twilio for SMS presented their own challenges, the most time-consuming aspect was perfecting the booking algorithm. In the end, I introduced overflow to timeslots and incorporated specific calculations to ensure the booking system was both flexible and tailored to the needs of car detailing businesses.

  • Pre-Launch Adjustments

    (October 2023)

    As we approach the launch, we're rigorously squashing minor bugs and refining user experiences.
