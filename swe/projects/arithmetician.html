Arithmetician

Arithmetician is an enhanced arithmetic training platform, tailored for those aiming to sharpen their mental math abilities, from finance professionals preparing for interviews to students gearing up for exams. Built on a Ruby on Rails backend and a React frontend, the platform not only challenges users with rapid arithmetic problems but also stores their scores in a PostgreSQL database. Users can review their progress by accessing their historical scores, selectively remove results if they believe an attempt was non-representative, and even revisit the exact question prompts they tackled.

  • Origin of Idea

    During my tenure as a portfolio manager, I delved into derivatives in the stock market. Recognizing the potential in Iran's stock options market, I developed a webscraper to spot arbitrage opportunities. With limited algorithmic trades and companies occasionally issuing attractively priced options, I leveraged the tool to quadruple my portfolio in a single year. Encouraged by a mentor, I set my sights on becoming a quantitative financial analyst (which I obviously didn't become because building software is more rewarding). A crucial requirement? Lightning-fast mental math. Despite a thorough search, platforms that catered to tracking progress were absent, leading me to rely on Zetamac and manual spreadsheet entries.

  • Thoughts on Ruby on Rails

    Having previously spent six months with Ruby on Rails at Yuhu, I was familiar with the framework but building an entire project from the ground up using the framework was a fresh challenge. Honestly, it felt like magic at times! For my personal projects, I gravitate towards frameworks where I have more control over the code. That said, seeing how powerful and efficient Rails can be, I totally get why top-notch companies opt for it. If a future project called for Ruby on Rails, I'd definitely jump back in.
